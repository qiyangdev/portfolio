---
title: How I Read
description: A brief note on my approach to reading.
date: 2026-06-14
author: Qiyang
---

For casual reading, I usually download epub files from Z-Library and read them
in the Books app on my iPhone. Books like _The Plum in the Golden Vase_ and
_Deng Xiaoping and the Transformation of China_ were all finished this way. In
my view, these books don't need a quiet environment or any solemn ritual. When I
read them, I don't expect to walk away with something obvious the moment I close
the cover—I simply want to read widely without digging too deep.

For technical books—such as _Professional JavaScript_ and _Build a LLM_—I start
by skimming "About this book" (if there is one) and selecting the sections I
need. For each chapter, I do a quick pass first and mark points that interest
me; this usually doesn't take long. Then I read carefully, rewriting and
recording the parts that matter in a note-taking app (a method I picked up from
_How to Take Smart Notes_). For any code involved (there's usually a starter
repo), I type it out by hand. Finally, I work through the extension exercises or
extra reading (most tutorial-style books include them), which often leads to
more sources—I try to go through those as thoroughly as I can and capture my
thoughts in notes.
